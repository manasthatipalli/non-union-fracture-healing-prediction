Predictive Model development to Identifing failed Healing in Patients after non-union Fraction

Project Abstract

Bone fractures are one of the most common musculoskeletal injuries and require timely diagnosis to ensure appropriate medical treatment and prevent complications. Traditionally, fracture detection from X-ray images relies on manual examination by radiologists and orthopedic specialists. However, manual diagnosis can be time-consuming and subject to human error, particularly when dealing with large volumes of medical imaging data. Recent advancements in machine learning and deep learning techniques have enabled the development of automated systems that assist in medical image analysis and improve diagnostic efficiency. The existing research focuses on predicting failed healing after non-union fracture surgery using machine learning models such as Logistic Regression, Support Vector Machines, and XGBoost based on structured clinical datasets. While these approaches demonstrate promising predictive capabilities, they rely on tabular patient data and do not directly analyze radiographic images for fracture detection. In this work, a deep learning–based fracture detection system is proposed to automatically classify bone X-ray images as fractured or non-fractured. The proposed model utilizes a transfer learning approach based on the ResNet50 convolutional neural network pretrained on ImageNet. The pretrained backbone is used as a feature extractor, followed by custom classification layers consisting of Global Average Pooling, fully connected layers, dropout regularization, and a sigmoid output layer for binary classification. The model is trained on a publicly available bone fracture dataset containing 9,463 X-ray images and evaluated using training, validation, and testing datasets. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed model achieves an overall test accuracy of 69%, with an F1-score of 0.71 for fractured cases and 0.67 for non-fractured cases. The developed system provides an automated framework for assisting healthcare professionals in preliminary fracture detection and highlights the potential of deep learning methods for improving medical image analysis in orthopedic diagnosis.
